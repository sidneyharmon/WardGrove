About Us

Ward Grove Farm has been in our family since the 1830's growing cotton, soybeans, corn, and livestock. In 1986 the first Christmas trees were planted. In 1990, choose and cut sales began with a farm wagon at the center of operations. With the addition of our rustic cabin and mule-driven wagon rides in 1993, families began looking to our farm as an important Christmas tradition.

We are open from 9 until dark on Thursdays, Fridays, and Saturdays and noon until dark on Sundays. We will be open by appointment on Monday through Wednesday. We will cut your tree, shake the needles and bale it for easy handling. All children receive a candy cane as well as a coloring book.

On weekends, your family can enjoy riding in a mule-driven wagon to select your tree.

When you have made your selection, come back to the log cabin for complimentary hot chocolate and cider.

Our Products

We grow two species of pine trees, Virginia and White Pine.

The needles of the Virginia Pine are soft and range in length from 1.5 to 3 inches in length. This tree has a pleasant pine fragrance and good needle retention.

The Eastern White Pine has been grown as a Christmas tree in the U.S. for many years. The needles of this tree are soft and flexible, blue green in color, and 2.5 to 5 inches in length. Needle retention is excellent. The White Pine has a mild pleasant fragrance.
